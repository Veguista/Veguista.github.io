Aim Assist

Isometric bullet-hell games need Aim Assist to compensate for the players' distorted visual perception. But my project had special needs:

Usually : Single Target

Games like Hades or Tunic have less enemies on screen and a single source for player attacks. Single-target aim-assist works great for them, forcing players to focus on one enemy at a time in combat.

In my game, there were more enemies and players fired multiple weapons at once. With a regular aim-assist system, bullet trajectories would converge at the first enemy, then diverge and scatter after it. Together with the use of projectiles, players would miss most of their attacks.

My System : Crowd Target

I created another aim-assist system: Instead of attacking one enemy, it defined a point for the weapon trajectories to converge in. This point was a weighted average (by proximity) of the position of enemies in front of the player.

With this new system, groups of enemies were easier to hit and the number of missed bullets was reduced significantly. It also gave more control to players since it allowed for a less snappy aim-assist.
